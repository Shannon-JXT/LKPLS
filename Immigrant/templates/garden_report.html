Our Review About Community Gardens

Community gardens are great places for growing traditional food, learning through cultural exchange, and also relaxing and making new friends. They are considered as great assets that are operating on the public land of Melbourne encouraging the wide range of community to take part in its activities and management. People of all ages, gender, and culture having different language backgrounds can be part of it.

The community garden concept is believed to be a good initiative as it helps to bring diverse inhabitants together and also results in reducing the crime rates by enhancing the visibility among the neighborhood. These multi-cultural gardens have always been considered as a crucial contributor to settling people in a new country by providing the feeling of "being at home".

Aim of Community Gardens:

  • They are granted as an inventive way for people from different backgrounds to grow their traditional food and can have cultural exchange among the gardeners.
  • To unite people from all walks of life by fostering an enthusiastic connected community.
  • Help to promote the advantages of gardening and also Melbourne city's agriculture. Check out more Information on Victorian Community Gardens. How it contributes to Community and Social Life!
  • Community gardens or multi-cultural Gardens can help immigrant families in disadvantaged situations. It provides access to natural products, an opportunity to connect with nature and also a meeting place for the neighborhood.
  • It provides comprehensive meeting points where inhabitants of different ages and cultures can meet up and share their experiences.
  • It helps to reduce cultural barriers by fostering cross-cultural interaction as it promotes a significant understanding of different cultural groups by the participation of a broader range of people having different cultures.
  • Assist in fostering a sense of community and belongingness.
  • It helps to raise the physical as well as mental health of communities with different habitats.
  • Provides a platform for people to learn and exchange information of different cultural gardening style and cooking. To learn more, visit Community Gardens benefits.
